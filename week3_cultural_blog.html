Thoughts On Pairing



What Was It Like To Pair With Others To Solve Challenges?

I love pairing with classmates on challenges. I think it makes the process of combing through errors much easier when I have a partner present. There's nothing like another set of eyes to catch things I might ordinarily miss when working alone. I love getting to know everyone I pair with during the check-in phase of our session.


Was Something Particularly Fun Or Rewarding?

One of my partners figured out a way to draw a map in Stypi for our create your own code combat mission challenge. I thought it was extremely creative and we had a blast making changes to our map and were cracking up over thinking of names for our character.


Was Something Frustrating And Difficult For You?

Not particularly, although, there are times when it becomes apparent that my partner and I are not as "rabit-hole resistant" as we should be in order to make our session as efficient as possible.



Thoughts On Feedback



How Did You Feel When You Read Your Feedback?

No surprises. I felt like the feedback I got was honest and fair and reflected well upon me.


Was The Feedback You Received Helpful To Your Learning?

In the most recent instance, only slightly. I haven't had a session in which I felt that enough went wrong for there to be much to elaborate on in terms of suggestions for how I might improve my strategies for learning. The only thing it seems I need to work on so far is pseudocoding to optimize communication between my partner and I.


Based On The Feedback You Received, What Are You Going To Do To Improve Next Time You Pair?

Next time I pair, I need to be mindful of how I communicate ideas from the start. I should work on my partner on describing the strategy we will use to solve the challenge in plain English and make sure we are both on the same page.


How Was It To Write Feedback?

Writing feedback is generally very easy for me. It can be a little difficult to provide something actionable to my partner if our session went really well so, in these cases, I just try to tell them what I liked about our session in particular and suggest that they continue to do it in the future.


What Was Most Difficult For You When Writing Feedback For Your Pair?

Thinking of actionable feedback is hard when the session goes perfectly well. I try my best to make sure my feedback is as robust as possible so that my pair can get a lot out of it. Sometimes I feel a bit verbose.


Overall, What Do You Think Of Using Pairing And Feedback To Guide Your Learning?

I like that feedback gives me a great deal of insight into how I can improve as a developer. I will be keeping eyes on how frequently I am told I was driving throughout my sessions as this is an area in which I am agressively trying to grow.
